# Tree Services — Intake Call Script

One script. GL is the backbone. WC and Auto are separate highlighted sections you bolt on based on what they need.

## How to Read This

- Everything without a tag = ask on every call (GL minimum)
- `[WC]` = Workers Comp only — separate section below
- `[AUTO]` = Commercial Auto only — separate section below
- `[IM]` = Inland Marine / equipment only
- `[GLZ-APP-96]` = fills a specific field on the Scottsdale Tree Trimmers supplemental
- `[SIMON]` = question Simon asked on Courtesy Cuts call — proven to work in conversation

**Target time:** 15-20 min. Every shortcut costs 3-7 days in UW round-trips.

**Sources:** Simon's Courtesy Cuts intake (32 questions), Scottsdale GLZ-APP-96 Tree Trimmers GL Application, ACORD 125/126/127/129/130/146.

## 1. OPEN — Take the Handoff (30 sec)

Referral agent introduces the lead. Listen, then take over.

> "Got it, thanks [referral agent]. I'll take it from here."

> "Hey [name], how's it going? I'm [your name] with Soma Insurance. We work with a lot of tree service companies so you're in good hands. Let me grab a few details and I'll get you quotes as fast as I can."

**[Q1]** "Let me confirm — the business is [name]? Can you spell that for me?"

**[Q2]** "And the owner's name — can you spell that?"

**[Q3]** "What's the best address for the business — where you operate out of?"

**[Q4]** "Just in case we get disconnected — best number to call you back?"

**[Q5]** "And best email?"

**[Q6]** "Quick question — are you currently working with any other insurance broker on this?"

## 2. DISCOVER — Let Them Talk First (3-4 min)

Don't jump to the checklist. Start open.

> "So tell me about the business — what kind of tree work do you do?"

*Listen. They'll usually tell you trimming, removal, stumps, how many trucks. Let them finish, then fill gaps with the questions below.*

**[Q7]** `[SIMON]` "Do you guys do any landscaping work, or is it just tree trimming?"
*Opens the operations conversation naturally. Courtesy Cuts: just tree trimming, planning to add landscaping in 2-3 years.*

**[Q8]** `[SIMON]` "Could you just give me a walk-through of what you guys are looking for? Any challenges you're having?"
*Let them vent. Courtesy Cuts was paying $9K/month — this is where you find the pain point.*

**[Q9]** `[GLZ-APP-96]` "How long have you been in business?"

**[Q10]** `[GLZ-APP-96]` "And how many years of experience in tree work total — including before this company?"
*Experience in the field ≠ years the business has existed. Carriers want both.*

**[Q11]** "Is this an LLC, corporation, sole prop?"

**[Q12]** "Do you have your federal tax ID handy?"
*If no: "No worries — text or email it when you get a chance."*

**[Q13]** `[GLZ-APP-96]` "Is this full-time or part-time?"

**[Q14]** "Do you have a website?"

**[Q15]** "Any DBA or other name the business goes by?"

## 3. OPERATIONS — The Core of the Call (5-6 min)

This is where deals live or die. Every question here feeds the Scottsdale GLZ-APP-96 operations table and the ACORD 126.

### Type of work + revenue breakdown

> "Let me walk through the types of tree work — just tell me what applies and roughly what percentage of revenue."

**[Q16]** `[SIMON]` "He's doing tree trimming, pruning, I'm assuming. Right?"
If yes: "What percentage of revenue?" `[GLZ-APP-96: Tree trimming — payroll + receipts]`

**[Q17]** `[SIMON]` "Is he doing any tree removal?"
If yes: "What percentage?" `[GLZ-APP-96: Tree/stump removal — payroll + receipts]`
*Courtesy Cuts: about 10%.*

**[Q18]** `[SIMON]` "Is he doing stump grinding?"
**[Q19]** `[SIMON]` "Do you know what percentage?"
`[GLZ-APP-96: Tree/stump removal — combined with Q17 or broken out]`
*Courtesy Cuts: 20%.*

**[Q20]** `[SIMON]` "No lawn care and excavation work, right?"
`[GLZ-APP-96: Landscaping — payroll + receipts]` `[GLZ-APP-96: Lawn Servicing — payroll + receipts]`

**[Q21]** `[SIMON]` "Is he doing any emergency or storm cleanup?"
*Florida companies: "We live in Florida, so hurricane season, pretty much that's part of it." Note it even if they don't track it separately.*

**[Q22]** `[GLZ-APP-96]` "Any controlled burns or land clearing?"

**[Q23]** `[GLZ-APP-96]` "Highway, street, or utility right-of-way maintenance?"

**[Q24]** `[GLZ-APP-96]` "Any logging and lumbering — cutting timber for sale?"

**[Q25]** `[GLZ-APP-96]` "Do you make and sell mulch from what you cut?"

**[Q26]** `[GLZ-APP-96]` "Any crop dusting, aerial spraying, or defensible space work?"
*Almost always no. Confirm and move on.*

**[Q27]** `[GLZ-APP-96]` "Is anyone on the team ISA-certified — a certified arborist?"
`[GLZ-APP-96: Arborist — ISA certified Y/N, payroll + receipts]`
*Positive underwriting factor. Better rates if yes.*

**[Q28]** `[GLZ-APP-96]` "Any power generation — other than emergency backup?"
*Rare. Confirm no and move on.*

*Percentages need to total 100%. Round to nearest 10% if they can't be exact.*

### Commercial vs residential

**[Q29]** `[SIMON]` `[GLZ-APP-96]` "Is he doing more like commercial work, residential work? What's the split?"
*Courtesy Cuts: 100% residential. Scottsdale GLZ-APP-96 Q5 needs the percentage split.*

### Geography

**[Q30]** `[SIMON]` "Where are you guys located?" / "What states do you operate in?"
`[GLZ-APP-96: States/Areas of operation]`

**[Q31]** `[GLZ-APP-96]` "Any current or future work planned in New York?"
If yes:
- "Do New York operations exceed 50% of total receipts?"
- "Any operations in the five boroughs — Bronx, Brooklyn, Manhattan, Queens, Staten Island?"
*Labor Law 240/241. NYC boroughs are near-impossible to place.*

### Height, climbing, and cranes

**[Q32]** `[SIMON]` "Do you know the maximum height of trees he's working on?"
*UWs ask this. Courtesy Cuts: Robert didn't know. Come back to it if needed.*

**[Q33]** `[SIMON]` "Does he climb on the trees?"
**[Q34]** `[SIMON]` "So he subs that out?" *(if they mention a tree climber)*
*Courtesy Cuts: climbing subbed out to a subcontractor. This matters for both GL and WC.*

**[Q35]** `[SIMON]` `[GLZ-APP-96]` "Is he doing any crane-assisted tree removal?"
If yes: "What's the maximum height?"
*Scottsdale GLZ-APP-96 Q7. Courtesy Cuts: contracts crane work out. Significantly higher hazard.*

### Chemicals

**[Q36]** `[GLZ-APP-96]` "Do you use any pesticides or herbicides?"
If yes:
- "Are they EPA approved?"
- "What percentage of operations?"
*Scottsdale GLZ-APP-96 Q6. Pollution exposure.*

### Proximity hazards

**[Q37]** `[SIMON]` "Do you know if he's doing any work near power lines, public roads, or buildings or structures?"
*Simon asked all three in one shot. Courtesy Cuts: residential, so buildings yes, power lines occasionally, no main roads.*

### Safety

**[Q38]** `[GLZ-APP-96]` "Do you have a formal safety program in place?"
*Scottsdale GLZ-APP-96 Q8. Positive factor for GL and WC.*

## 8. WORKERS COMP — `[WC]` (3-4 min)

> *Skip entirely if they don't need WC.*

> "Let me ask a few things specifically for the workers comp quote."

### WC class codes for tree services:
- *0106 — Tree pruning/trimming (no removal)*
- *2702 — Logging/lumbering*
- *2710 — Tree removal (including stump)*
- *Payroll split by operation type matters — trimming vs removal are different codes and different rates.*

**[Q70]** `[SIMON]` "Do you know by any chance what carrier he's with for workers comp?"
*Courtesy Cuts: setting up WC through Gusto payroll service. Simon immediately flagged this — "Gusto is not an insurance carrier expert. Let me take care of the comp."*

**[Q71]** `[SIMON]` "Is it with ADP by any chance?" *(or Gusto, or through payroll)*
*Why: Payroll-bundled WC is almost always overpriced and under-covered. If they're getting WC through a PEO or payroll provider, that's an easy win — we can beat it.*

**[Q72]** "Do you know your experience modification rate — your e-mod?"
*Below 1.0 = discount. Above 1.0 = surcharge. If they don't know, we look it up through NCCI.*

**[Q73]** "Have you had any WC audits? Any audit disputes or additional premium owed?"
*Unpaid audit balances block placement.*

**[Q74]** "Do you have owners or officers on payroll? Who, and annual compensation?"
*Owners can be included or excluded. State rules vary. Need names, titles, ownership %.*

**[Q75]** "Any employees under 16 or over 60?"

**[Q76]** "Any seasonal or temporary workers?"
*Tree services are often seasonal. Affects payroll estimates and audit exposure.*

**[Q77]** "Do any employees work for another business?"

**[Q78]** "Does payroll include overtime? Rough percentage?"
*WC carriers often exclude overtime premium from rated payroll.*

**[Q79]** "Do you provide group transportation — driving the crew to job sites together?"
*Creates additional WC exposure if employees are injured in transit.*

**[Q80]** "Do any employees travel out of state for work?"
*May need Part 3 other-states coverage.*

**[Q81]** "Do your subs carry their own workers comp?"
*If subs don't carry WC, their payroll gets picked up on YOUR policy at audit. Courtesy Cuts subs 50% — this is critical.*

If WC claims: **[Q82]** "What was the nature of the injury — fall from a tree, chainsaw accident, struck by a branch?"
*Tree service WC claims are often severe. Type of injury matters for carrier appetite.*

### WC documents to request:
- WC loss runs (separate from GL if different carrier)
- Experience mod worksheet
- Prior WC policy dec page

## Sales Instinct — What Simon Did Right

These aren't questions but they moved the deal:

1. *Built rapport fast.* "I'm actually in Florida too." "I went to UCF." Mirror their world.
2. *Anchored on price immediately.* "I have a client doing similar volume, they're only paying $12-13K a year." Robert's guy was paying $108K/year. The gap sells itself.
3. *Named the problem.* "They're just taking advantage of him, whoever his agent is." Puts you on their side.
4. *Promised the outcome.* "I'm gonna save you guys 50% or more." Bold but backed by the comparable.
5. *Pushed bundling.* "Let me take care of the comp and I'll bundle everything for you guys." More lines = stickier client.
6. *Closed on urgency.* "How quickly do you need coverage?" Got "end of the week" — now you have a deadline to work toward.
7. *Asked about the consultant's business.* "In case my clients ever need, I'll shoot them your way." Turns the gatekeeper into a referral source.

## Objection Handling

**"How much is this going to cost?"**
> "I can't give you an exact number without running it through the markets, but I have tree service clients doing similar volume paying way less than you'd think. I'll have real numbers within [timeline]."

**"I'm paying $X a month and that seems like a lot."**
> "Let me take a look at what you're getting for that. A lot of times tree service guys end up with policies scattered across three or four carriers because the last agent didn't bundle them. Let me see what we can do."
*Courtesy Cuts: $9K/month across 6 carriers. Consolidation alone saves money.*

**"I just need a COI."**
> "Totally get it. To issue a certificate we need an active policy first. Let me get you quoted and bound — COI same day."

**"I don't have my VINs / driver info / equipment serials right now."**
> "No worries. Just text or email when you have it. I'll start with what we have. Email is angie@somainsure.com."

**"My payroll company handles workers comp."**
> "I see that a lot — Gusto, ADP, they bundle it in. But they're not insurance specialists. We can almost always beat the rate and get you better coverage. Let me quote it and you can compare."
