Soma Insurance Services · All bound deals through April 2026 · Prepared April 9, 2026
Executive Summary

What Separates a $15K Deal from a $500 One

We analyzed all 43 won deals to identify the patterns that predict high-premium accounts at first contact. Here are the five findings that matter most.

1

Three industries account for 90% of premium revenue. Construction/trades, security guard services, and restaurant/bar generated $330K of $356K total. Everything else combined contributed $26K across 12 deals.

2

Labor spend is the single strongest predictor. Combined W-2 payroll + subcontractor costs. Deals with $200K+ labor spend averaged $21K premium with a 100% hit rate for crossing $3K. Deals with $0 averaged $1,800.

3

Revenue only predicts premium in high-hazard industries. A $1.2M wig store wrote $471. A $120K security company wrote $15,260. Filter to construction/security/food and $1M+ revenue hits $3K+ 100% of the time.

4

Multi-line deals are nearly automatic $3K+ accounts. Leads needing GL+WC or any combination crossed $3K 90% of the time, averaging $14,200. Single-line GL in low-hazard trades averaged $1,100.

5

Vendor lead data is wrong on the fields that matter most. Payroll, employee count, and revenue from Tivly/Bold Penguin were materially inaccurate in over half of deals. The intake call is the only reliable source for qualification.

Key Finding

When labor spend is $100K+ in a high-hazard trade, the deal hits $3K+ every single time regardless of revenue. Labor spend is the dominant variable; revenue only matters when labor is low.

Section 3

Practical Application

The Three Questions That Matter on an Intake Call

1
"What does your business do?"

Construction, security, or restaurant/bar = proceed. Anything else = likely sub-$2K unless specialty line.

2
"Between your W-2 payroll and what you pay subcontractors, how much total are you spending on labor annually?"

$100K+ = fast-track, guaranteed $3K+. $200K+ = high-priority, average $21K deal.

3
"What types of coverage do you need?"

GL+WC or any multi-line combo = 90% chance of $3K+. Single GL = weaker.

Vendor lead data from Tivly, Bold Penguin, and Seal was materially wrong on the fields that drive qualification in over half of verified deals.

Section 6

Methodology

  • Data sources: Supabase leads table, lead interactions (call transcripts, emails, texts), and Slack #wins channel.
  • Interaction-first verification: For every deal with a matching lead, business details were verified against intake call transcripts. When vendor lead data conflicted with information from calls, the call data was used.
  • Labor spend: W-2 payroll + 1099 contractor payments + subcontractor costs. Where exact sub costs were not stated but operations were described as "100% subcontracted," context was noted rather than estimated.
  • Unmatched deals: 4 deals had no matching lead in Supabase (Best Restoration Solutions, Arseas Security, The Good Flea, Brewer Plumbing). Included in totals but excluded from variable-level analysis.
  • Premium figures: Annual unless noted. ArmorPoint's DB entry showed a 6-month figure; the #wins channel confirmed the annual placement at $10,094 through RT/NICO.
